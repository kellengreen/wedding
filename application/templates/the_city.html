For those of you that might enjoy a bit of sightseeing while you are here in San Francisco please see a few ideas of fun things to see while you are here. Please note the week of October 18th, there are a few very large events being hosted in San Francisco so hotel rooms will be limited, we recommend booking well in advance. Please see a great guide to the city here! A few of our favorites are listed below.

Alcatraz

An amazing tour of the historical state prison. Make sure to book tickets in advance as the tour sells out in advance.

Presidio

Spend some time getting to know one of the most beautiful National Parks in the nation. There are many self guided tours and wonderful bike/walking paths to explore.

North Beach/ Pier 39

Where we call home, we have lived in the North Beach neighborhood since moving to SF in 2011. We live right at the base of beautiful Coit Tower and up the hill from Saint’s Peter and Paul Church. Looking for a delicious breakfast? Head to Mama’s- you will be sure to wait but it is said to have the best breakfast in town. While in the area make sure to check out Fisherman’s Wharf on pier 39 to take a look at the sea lions, beautiful views, and aquatic park.

City Tour with Vantigo

Our great friends Erik and Amy Horman have launched a fabulously unique tour company here in San Francisco. Lillie, a 1971 Volkswagen Transporter Deluxe will take you on a 7 x 7 San Francisco adventure, a City Lights Night Tour, or out on an Oyster trip never to forget! For those of you who haven’t spent much time in San Francisco or simply want a lovely day with the best tour guide in the city, check out Vantigo! Make sure to book early as Lillie has a limited number of seats and her owners will be joining us at the party on Saturday evening!

Bike Rentals

A fun activity and great way to cover some ground here in SF! You can ride over the Golden Gate Bridge, tool around in Sausalito, and take the ferry back to the city.

Cable Car Ride

A few dollars in San Fran can buy you a roller coaster ride of the city. San Francisco's cable cars are a cute relic of a time before buses and cars – and they give a great retro-style tour of the city's hills, and neighborhoods: Union Square, Chinatown, Fisherman's Wharf and the other districts.

Wine Country

One of the best parts of living in the Bay Area is our proximity to beautiful Sonoma and Napa wine country. An easy drive over the Golden Gate will take you to a lovely day of sipping on some of our favorite vino!
